What is Black Mesa?

The Black Mesa Research Facility is a world-class research complex located deep in the New Mexico desert, constructed by repurposing an old missile silo installation.
While it is known publicly as an Air Force base, the internal operations span a vast range of pure and applied sciences.

Research Areas

  • Theoretical Physics and Applied Mathematics
  • Radiation and Nuclear Research
  • Laser Engineering and Hydraulics
  • Robotics and Genetics
  • Zoology and Biology
  • Chemistry and Advanced Materials
  • Hydropower and Energy Studies

Additionally, we contribute to future space science through HEV suit development, rocket and satellite launch testing, and the reuse of decommissioned ICBM launch facilities.

Leadership History

  • Dr. Reagan Strauss (1955–1966)

    Founding director, known for authoritative leadership.
  • Dr. Kalevi Pulkkinen (1966–1992)

    Most successful administrator in terms of research efficiency and internal trust.
  • Dr. Wallace Breen (1992–Present)

One of the Few Entrances

Access to the Black Mesa Facility is highly restricted. Only a few certified checkpoints exist for authorized entry.

Government Collaboration

  • DOE (Department of Energy): Oversees nuclear, radiation, and reactor security.
  • DOD (Department of Defense): Supports military technology research and rocket testing.

The U.S. Marine Corps and DOE security forces ensure operational safety.

Self-Sufficient Operations

  • Security: Internal police, SSF, and FRTOU tactical units
  • Fire & Rescue: Dedicated firefighting and emergency teams
  • Medical: Hospital, psychiatric care, emergency treatment
  • Infrastructure: In-house power plants, airfields, logistics (BMTA), and aviation authority (BMAA)
  • Support: Internal cafeterias, dormitories, and catering services
